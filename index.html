What is Autism?

Autism spectrum disorder (ASD) is a brain development condition that affects how a person perceives and interacts socially with others. This leads to difficulties in communication and social interaction. The disorder also includes restricted and repetitive patterns of behavior. The term "spectrum" in ASD refers to the wide range of symptoms and their varying degrees of severity Autism spectrum disorder includes conditions that were previously thought to be separate: autism, Asperger's syndrome, childhood vanishing disorder, and a form of pervasive developmental disorder not otherwise specified.

Is there a cure for it?

There is no cure for autism spectrum disorder. However, early intervention and treatment, especially during the preschool years, can make a significant difference in the lives of many children with this disorder.
